LEARN WHAT IS THE RECIPE AND PROCESS OF MAKING PANDESAL


Let's Learn!

RECIPE'S

  • 4 cups (500 g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/4 tsp (7 g) active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1/3 cup (65–70 g) granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup (240 ml) warm milk (about 105–115°F / 40–46°C)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 2–4 tbsp warm water (only if dough is too dry)
  • Fine dry breadcrumbs for coating
  • Melted butter for brushing (optional)

PROCESS

  1. roof yeast: Stir 1–2 tsp sugar into the warm milk, sprinkle yeast on top, wait 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix dry: In a large bowl, whisk flour, remaining sugar, and salt.
  3. Combine: Make a well, add foamy yeast mixture and the egg, then add butter. Mix until a shaggy dough forms; add up to 2–4 tbsp warm water only if needed.
  4. Knead: Knead by hand 8–10 minutes (or 5–7 minutes in a stand mixer) until smooth and elastic.
  5. First rise: Place dough in a greased bowl, cover, let rise 1–1.5 hours at warm room temperature until doubled.
  6. Shape: Punch down dough, divide into ~18 pieces (≈40–45 g each). Shape into balls, roll lightly in breadcrumbs, place on baking sheets ~1" apart.
  7. Second rise: Cover and let rise 30–45 minutes until puffy.
  8. Bake: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake 12–15 minutes until golden.
  9. Finish: Brush with melted butter if desired. Cool slightly before serving
